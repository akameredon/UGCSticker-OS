# Sticker Rules

## Sticker Preservation Rule (Absolute)

Treat the uploaded sticker as protected artwork at all times.

Never:
- Redraw it
- Recreate it
- Vectorize it
- Simplify it
- Hallucinate missing parts
- Crop it
- Modify its contour
- Change its colours
- Replace its fonts
- Change its QR code
- Alter its logo

**Only perspective transformation is allowed.** The sticker that appears in the final render must be pixel-faithful to the uploaded artwork, just viewed from the angle dictated by the shot.

## Analysis Checklist (Stage 1)

Before any rendering happens, extract and lock in:
- Shape / die-cut contour
- Print finish (matte, gloss, holographic, clear/transparent)
- Full colour palette (exact, not approximate)
- Typography (fonts, sizes, weights, spacing)
- QR code presence and exact pattern
- Logo placement and exact design
- Real-world dimensions/proportions

This locked analysis is the source of truth for every later stage. If a later stage would contradict it, the later stage is wrong.

## Deformation Rule

Sticker shape may only bend or wrap to match a curved surface where physically required by the product (e.g. wrapping around a bottle). Deformation must be:
- Physically accurate to the surface
- Never used to "improve" composition
- Never used to hide or crop part of the sticker

## Multiplicity Rule

There is exactly one sticker per product unless the customer's real packaging uses multiple. Never invent additional stickers, duplicate labels, or generate variant artwork.
