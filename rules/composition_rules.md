# Composition Rules

## Hero Rule

The hand-held sticker (or sticker-on-product, depending on shot type) is the hero of the frame:
- Occupies roughly **40%** of the image
- Always faces the camera
- Always perfectly readable
- Entire die-cut contour visible
- No fingers, props, or other elements covering important text or the logo
- Full contour preserved, uncropped

## Container Rule

The product/container:
- Occupies roughly **20%** of the frame
- Sticker is applied naturally to it — no floating, no oversized or undersized placement relative to the container's real proportions
- Sticker fits within its natural boundary (e.g. inside the lid edge, within the label panel)
- Natural reflections and shadows consistent with the material
- Manufacturing-accurate placement (see `manufacturing_rules.md`)

## Character Rule

The approved UGC model:
- Never changes identity between renders for the same customer
- Same hairstyle, same face, same smile
- Same wardrobe, unless the user explicitly requests a change
- Natural Nigerian appearance and setting
- Authentic UGC posture and framing — this should read as a real person's phone photo/video, not a staged studio ad

## Background Rule

Background depends on the customer's industry. Use the matching file in `/templates/` (Restaurant, Juice, Cosmetics, Food, Fashion, Pharmaceutical, Household, or General) for the specific scene direction. In every case:
- The customer's product and the model remain the visual hero
- Background elements support the story but never compete with the sticker or product for attention
- Background must be believable for the real-world setting the product is sold or used in

## Layout Priority

When elements compete for space, priority order is:
1. Sticker readability (hero)
2. Product/container realism
3. Model authenticity
4. Background believability
