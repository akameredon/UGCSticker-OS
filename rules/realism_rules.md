# Realism Rules

## Material Physics

Every surface must behave like its real-world material:
- Glass/plastic: correct transparency, refraction, and reflection
- Glossy sticker finish: specular highlight consistent with light source
- Matte sticker finish: soft, diffused reflection only
- Metal (caps, foil seals): accurate specular highlights and environment reflection

## Skin & People

- Natural, unfiltered skin texture — visible pores, natural tone variation, no plastic/AI-smooth skin
- Realistic hand anatomy when holding the sticker or product (this is a common failure point — check finger count, joint bends, and grip realism)
- Natural eye contact with camera for UGC-style authenticity, unless the shot calls for a candid look-away

## Shadows & Grounding

- Every object casts a shadow consistent with the scene's light source direction and intensity
- Products must appear physically grounded on the surface they rest on — no floating, no impossible contact points

## Environmental Consistency

- Reflections in glass/metal must match the actual background of the scene, not a generic studio reflection
- Depth of field must be consistent with the stated camera/lens rules in `camera_rules.md`

## Anti-Uncanny Checklist

Before finalizing, check for and eliminate:
- Warped or extra fingers
- Melted or duplicated logos/text
- Inconsistent lighting direction between subject and background
- Product proportions that don't match a real container of that type
